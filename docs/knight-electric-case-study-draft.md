### How a Tennessee electrical contractor put inspections, inventory, and compliance on autopilot

**Client:** Knight Electric Inc. — electrical contractor, Tennessee/Kentucky
**Built & run by:** Verdorian Technologies (QRSafePro platform)
**Scope:** Equipment safety inspections · shop & truck inventory · compliance records · office reporting

#### The problem

Safety inspections lived in binders. Material stock lived in someone's head. Every
audit meant a paper chase, and every missing tool or miscounted spool cost field
hours to run down.

- Inspection records: paper forms, manual filing, slow audit prep
- Inventory: no live count across [CONFIRM: # of storage locations/trucks]
- Compliance: OSHA-adjacent documentation assembled by hand

#### What we built

**QRSafePro** — a QR-code-driven inspection and inventory platform the crew uses
from their phones:

- **Scan-to-inspect:** every piece of equipment carries a QR label; inspections are
  logged in seconds with pass/fail history attached to the asset
- **PIN-verified material check-in/out:** who took what, when — with a fast PIN scan
  at the shop counter
- **Full materials catalog:** ~5,800 catalog materials imported and organized into
  labeled bins with printed Brady labels
- **Live dashboards:** stock levels, inspection status, and overdue items visible to
  the office in real time

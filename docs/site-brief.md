## 1. Positioning (one sharp line)

**For operations-heavy businesses, we build the automation your team actually uses — production systems for the inspections, compliance, and documentation work that can't afford to break.** _(High confidence.)_

Why this and not "we automate anything with AI": the broad ICP is real, but the *credible wedge* is operational/compliance/documentation work — that's exactly what the four shipped products prove (QRSafePro inspections, ChangeOrderAI change orders, PolicyPilot submissions, VoicePencil structured notes). The category is crowded with "we connect your apps with Zapier" shops; the demand data puts **operations (37%)** as the #1 automated function and **compliance (12%) + document management (8%)** close behind ([kissflow](https://kissflow.com/workflow/workflow-automation-statistics-trends/)). We own the hard, high-stakes end of that — and stay broad in *who* by leading with the workflow, not the industry.

**Supporting positioning lines (rotate as subheads):**
- "Automation that survives an audit."
- "We build the systems teams keep using after the demo's over."
- "The build is only half of it — we stay on to keep it sharp." _(maps Tier 3 Care to the industry's #1 differentiator, [digitalagencynetwork](https://digitalagencynetwork.com/ai-agency-pricing/))_

## 2. Core offer (outcome-framed) + risk reversal

**The wedge offer — lead with the $497 audit.** _(High confidence.)_ This is Verdorian's single strongest conversion asset: the category benchmark for an automation audit is **$5,000–$15,000** ([digitalagencynetwork](https://digitalagencynetwork.com/ai-agency-pricing/)). At **$497**, the audit is a near-frictionless "find out where the money is leaking" entry point that out-positions the entire field on risk. Frame it as the outcome, not the deliverable:

> **For $497, we find your three highest-ROI automations and hand you a build-ready plan with real effort estimates — before you commit to a single line of code.**

**Risk reversal / guarantee** — ✅ APPROVED FINAL (Zobayer, sign-off):
- **Ship exactly this:** **"We find at least 3 automation opportunities in your operation, or the audit's free."** Specific, countable, defensible for a $497 product.
- The research shows competitors gesture at "guarantee delivery" but publish no terms ([research-notes §4](research-notes.md)) — a *specific, small, honest* guarantee beats a vague one.

**3 proof elements (real, shipped — no invented metrics):**
1. **QRSafePro** — live web platform; QR equipment inspections that become permanent, audit-ready records. (The flagship "automation that survives an audit" proof.)
2. **ChangeOrderAI** — change orders drafted from plain language, priced, and routed for approval. (Proves the voice→structured-document→workflow capability.)
3. **PolicyPilot + VoicePencil** — submission-to-binding insurance pipeline; voice notes filed as finished work. (Proves breadth across regulated ops and mobile/iOS.)

Use the live product UIs as the demo. No client logos or counts are claimed (voice rule).

## 6. Conversion: events to track + A/B tests

**Events to instrument (PostHog):**
| Event | Where | Why |
|---|---|---|
| `cta_book_call_clicked` (prop: `location` = hero/offer/final/nav) | every CTA | which CTA position drives bookings |

| `service_tier_viewed` / `service_cta_clicked` (prop: tier) | Services | which tier pulls |
| `product_demo_viewed` (prop: product) | Products | which proof resonates |
| `faq_opened` (prop: question) | FAQ | which objection is hottest |
| `audit_offer_cta_clicked` | Offer band | does the $497 wedge convert |

**A/B tests, ranked by expected impact:**
1. **(Highest)** Hero CTA framing: **"Book a call"** vs **"Get your $497 audit"** as the primary button. The $497 wedge is the strongest differentiator vs. a $5K–$15K field — testing whether leading with price or with the soft call converts better is the single biggest lever. _(High confidence it matters; direction unknown — test it.)_
2. **(Medium-high)** Presence of the **Problem / cost-of-inaction** band after the hero (on vs. off). Every competitor pattern leads with a quantified problem; verify it lifts Verdorian's booking rate rather than just adding scroll.
3. **(Medium)** Risk-reversal line on the Offer band: **approved guarantee present** ("We find at least 3 automation opportunities in your operation, or the audit's free") vs **"no retainer, no lock-in"** only. Tests whether the explicit guarantee or the simple no-lock-in framing books more calls.

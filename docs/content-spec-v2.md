## A. SERVICES CATALOG

**Section eyebrow:** WHAT WE BUILD
**Section headline:** If a person does it every week, software can probably do it.
**Section subhead:** Nine ways we put repetitive work on autopilot — from the spreadsheet
that eats your mornings to the platform your whole operation runs on. Every one below is
something we have already built and shipped.

> **How the cards relate to the ladder.** These are *capabilities*, not packages. You don't
> buy a card — you start with the **$497 audit**, we find where these fit your operation,
> then build. The catalog answers "what can you actually do?"; the ladder answers "how do we
> work together?"

The cards group into three tiers of an operation, top to bottom — **the plumbing** (move and
connect data), **the work** (turn input into finished output), and **the platform** (software
your operation lives in). Scannable in ten seconds; honest in every line.

### Group 1 — The Plumbing — *moving and connecting your data*

#### 1. Workflow Automation
- **Card mark:** a single ink line that branches into three, each ending in a small gold node
  — the "one trigger, three actions" fork. Abstract, no gears, no robots.
- **Description:** We turn the multi-step routines that eat your week — approvals, hand-offs,
  reminders, status updates — into flows that run themselves. *For a business: the process
  moves at 2 a.m. without anyone babysitting it. For one person: the thing you keep forgetting
  to do just happens.*
- **Tools / proof:** n8n, Zapier, Make, and custom frontier-ai agents where the logic gets too
  smart for an off-the-shelf step.

#### 2. Integrations & Data Plumbing
- **Card mark:** two offset ink rectangles joined by a clean gold conduit — two systems, one
  pipe between them.
- **Description:** Your tools, spreadsheets, and accounts stop living on separate islands — we
  wire them into one flow so a record entered once shows up everywhere it should. *For a
  business: no more "which sheet is the real one." For one person: enter it once, never re-type
  it.*
- **Tools / proof:** the connective tissue inside every platform we ship — PolicyPilot moves a
  submission across intake, quoting, and binding without a single hand re-key.

#### 3. Data Entry & Reconciliation
- **Card mark:** a column of short ink ticks resolving into one clean gold checkmark — messy
  input, settled output.
- **Description:** The copy-paste, the re-typing, the line-by-line "do these two lists match"
  — handed to software, with validation baked in so errors get caught, not carried. *For a
  business: the reconciliation that took a person all Friday now takes a minute. For one person:
  the boring part is done before you sit down.*
- **Tools / proof:** the inventory engine behind QRSafePro's QRStock reconciles thousands of
  stock records this way today.

## C. WHY CHOOSE US

**Section eyebrow:** WHY WORK WITH US
**Section headline:** The quiet kind of good.
**Section subhead:** No hype, no "100% accuracy," no stranger's testimonials. Just a few honest
reasons people stay.

> Warmth and human insight in the register of a fine storyteller — clear, charming, true. No
> purple prose, no over-claims, no stats flex. Each reason is a short evocative headline plus
> one or two warm, precise sentences. The cumulative effect should be: *these are the people I
> want.*

### 1. We've actually done the work.
Anyone can promise automation; we can point to it running. Before we say "this can be done," we
have usually already done a harder version of it for someone else — and that quiet confidence
is the difference between a pitch and a craftsman.

### 2. We tell you the truth, even when it costs us the sale.
The industry is loud with "100% accurate" and "any industry, instantly." We'd rather say the
honest thing: some of your work should be automated, some shouldn't, and we'll tell you which is
which. It's a slower way to win a customer, and the only one that lets us keep you.

### 3. The first move is small, and it's on us if we're wrong.
You don't have to believe us to begin. Start with a **$497 audit**; if we can't find at least
three real ways to save your operation time, you owe nothing. We'd rather earn the build than
ask you to gamble on it.

### 4. We build for the messy real world, not the demo.
Software that only works in a clean demo is a magic trick. We build for the half-charged phone
on a job site, the spreadsheet nobody named properly, the way your team actually does the thing
at 4 p.m. on a Friday — because that's where the work really lives.

### 5. We don't disappear after launch.
The handoff is where most shops vanish. We stay — watching, tuning, adding the next automation
as your operation grows, through the **monthly Care** plan. A good system isn't delivered; it's
kept sharp. *(This closes the ladder: Care feeds the next audit.)*
